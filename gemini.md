wing names:

1. The Backgrounds: Warm Neutrals & "Alabaster"
What they are: The main background isn't a stark digital white; it’s a soft, slightly creamy, warm white.

Design Terms: Alabaster, Parchment, Warm Off-White, or Ivory.

Why it's used: It feels like premium textured paper, high-end packaging, or a clean artist workshop rather than a cold tech interface.

2. The Primary Theme: Forest Green & Deep Moss
What it is: The rich, dark greens used in the headers, buttons, and the sidebar of the Admin Panel.

Design Terms: Forest Green, Deep Moss, Hunter Green, or Juniper.

Why it's used: It immediately establishes a "fantasy forest," premium nature, or high-end tabletop gaming vibe (similar to classic library aesthetics or premium leather-bound books).

3. The Secondary Theme: Warm Earth & Timber tones
What it is: The deep brown gradients, borders, and UI accents used to frame the product cards and the file upload zones.

Design Terms: Wood Brown, Walnut, Burnt Umber, or Sepia.

Why it's used: It gives UI components a physical weight, making digital containers look like they are crafted out of actual wood, leather, or clay.

4. The Accents: Muted Gold & Amber
What it is: The glowing elements, active status tags, price quotes, and the mascot's eyes.

Design Terms: Warm Gold, Amber, Brass, or Ochre.

Why it's used: It provides a high-contrast pop against both the light backgrounds and dark green sidebars, signifying "premium quality," magical energy, or craftsmanship without looking loud or neon.

5. Dark Mode Base: Charcoal & Obsidian
What it is: The deep, matte dark background used on the Admin Panel control center.

Design Terms: Charcoal, Obsidian, or Gunmetal Gray.

Why it's used: It avoids pure black (#000000) to keep the layout feeling soft on the eyes, mimicking tactical manufacturing equipment or premium dark-mode software interfaces (like Razer or Synapse).
